How it works

Your image gets re-encoded with a Rec.2100 PQ color profile, the same signal HDR video uses. On HDR screens the bright parts render with the display's spare brightness, above normal white. Everything happens locally in your browser.

Where it survivesLinkedIn feed posts, as a JPEG, uploaded directly. Don't crop or edit in LinkedIn's composer, that strips the profile.
Where it diesScreenshots, re-saving in editors, WhatsApp and iMessage compression, copy-paste. Move the file itself.
Who sees itOnly people on HDR displays with battery saver off. Everyone else sees a normal image with slightly dimmer whites.
Best inputHigh-contrast graphics: logos, wordmarks, simple illustrations. Photo gradients can band at 8-bit.
