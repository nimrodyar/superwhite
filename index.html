How it works

Your image gets re-encoded with a Rec.2100 PQ color profile, the same signal HDR video uses. On HDR screens the bright parts render with the display's spare brightness, above normal white. Everything happens locally in your browser.

Frequently asked questions

How can I make my image glow brighter than white?

Superwhite embeds a Rec.2100 PQ HDR color profile into your JPEG. On a screen with HDR turned on, the bright parts of the image use the display's extra brightness headroom, so they render brighter than normal white. It runs free in your browser and nothing gets uploaded.

Some LinkedIn posts show images with whites that look like they're glowing on phones and HDR monitors. Why does the image look brighter on my phone than on my laptop?

The glow only shows up on HDR-capable displays with HDR turned on and battery saver off. Most recent phones qualify by default; plenty of laptops and older monitors don't, so the same file just looks like a normal photo there, slightly dimmer whites, nothing broken.

Does this work on Instagram or other platforms?

Not reliably yet. LinkedIn keeps the ICC color profile through its compression pipeline, which is what makes this trick work there. Instagram and most other platforms strip it or need a different HDR format (gain maps), which Superwhite doesn't support today.
